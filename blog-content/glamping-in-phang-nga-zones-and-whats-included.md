### Article
Riverside camping in Phang Nga isn't about roughing it. It's about trading your hotel room for a night's sleep alongside the Song Phrak river, with nothing between you and the jungle sounds except canvas walls and a proper bed. Our three glamping zones each offer a different setup, and knowing which one fits your group size and travel style makes all the difference.

## The Three Zones at a Glance

**Family Zone** — sleeps up to four or five guests — starts at THB 999 per night. This is the one to look at if you're travelling with children, grandparents, or a small group who wants to share one space without booking multiple tents. The Family Zone gives you room to move, room for a little extra gear, and the flexibility of having everyone together under one roof. It's also the most budget-friendly option per person when you've got a full house.

**Outdoor Zone** — sleeps two to three guests — starts at THB 1099 per night. Think of this as the sweet spot for couples or a trio of friends who want a balance of comfort and intimacy. It's a step up in privacy from the Family Zone, without the premium price tag of the Private Zone. There's enough room for a small table and chairs outside the tent — perfect for a morning coffee watching the river.

**Private Zone** — sleeps two guests only — starts at THB 1199 per night. This is our most secluded option, designed for couples wanting a quiet escape or anyone who values having a dedicated space entirely to themselves. The Private Zone sits slightly apart from the others, giving you that extra sense of distance from neighbouring campers.

## What's Included in Your Stay

All three zones come with the same core inclusions, so you're not trading comfort for price. Each booking gets you a proper tent with real bedding — no sleeping bags on foam mats here. You'll have access to shared bathroom facilities with hot showers, and there's a communal dining area where meals are served.

The stay types break down into three options. **Stay & Dine** gives you accommodation plus breakfast and dinner — the simplest choice if you just want to overnight and explore the area on your own. **Stay + Raft 5.5** pairs your night under canvas with our 5.5 kilometre white-water rafting adventure, including ziplining, lunch and a round-trip transfer from your hotel. That's the river experience bundled in with your sleep. And **Stay + Raft 7.5** does the same but with the extended 7.5 kilometre rafting route for those who want more rapids and a longer run.

If you're after the full package — [Raft All Day, Sleep by the River: The Overnight Combo](/en/blog/raft-all-day-sleep-by-the-river) — that's exactly what these stay types are built around. You spend the day on the water, come back to camp dirty and grinning, and wake up to the river in the morning. No packing up and moving hotels. No rush. Just the two best parts of the trip back to back.

## Choosing the Right Zone for You

The decision really comes down to group size and what kind of atmosphere you want.

Pick the **Family Zone** if you've got kids in tow, if you're a group of friends who don't mind sharing close quarters, or if you're trying to keep costs down while keeping everyone together. At THB 999 a night, it's genuinely affordable for a family of four compared to booking two hotel rooms in the area.

Pick the **Outdoor Zone** if you're a couple or a small group of three who wants a bit more breathing room than the Family Zone offers, but doesn't need complete solitude. The THB 1099 price point sits comfortably in the middle — enough privacy to feel like a proper getaway, enough community around the camp to meet other travellers if you want to.

Pick the **Private Zone** if seclusion is your priority — whether you're celebrating something, on a honeymoon, or just need quiet after days of busy Phuket beaches. At THB 1199 it's still modest compared to comparable glamping or boutique stays in the region, but it delivers that feeling of having the place to yourself.

One practical note: all our zones share the same facilities. So if you're in the Private Zone but the Outdoor Zone happens to be empty that night, you might find you have more space around you anyway. The zones describe your guaranteed sleeping area, but the camp as a whole tends to feel spacious regardless.

## FAQ

### Can I bring my own food to the camp?
Meals are included in your stay package, so there's no need to bring anything. The Stay & Dine option covers breakfast and dinner, while the Stay + Raft packages include lunch on your rafting day. Just show up hungry.

### Is the camping suitable for children?
Yes — the Family Zone is specifically designed for families and sleeps up to four or five guests. Children under 6 go free on the rafting portion when combined with an overnight stay, though they'll need their own bed in the tent.

### What should I pack for a night at the camp?
Light, breathable clothing, sturdy sandals or water shoes, a change of clothes for the next day, insect repellent, and a small daypack for your rafting trip. Bedding and towels are provided, so you don't need to bring those.

### Can I stay at the camp without doing the rafting?
Absolutely. The Stay & Dine option is exactly that — a night's accommodation with meals, no activity required. You can relax by the river, explore the surrounding jungle, or just hang out at camp while others do the rafting.
