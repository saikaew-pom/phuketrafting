### Article
There's something particular about drifting off to sleep with the sound of water sliding over stones, and waking up to jungle mist rising off the river. If you've done a day trip to Phang Nga for white-water rafting, you already know the thrill. But pairing it with an overnight at our riverside camp transforms the experience into something that stays with you longer than the adrenaline in your veins.

Here's what a full day and night with us actually looks like, from arrival to the morning after.

## Morning: From Your Hotel to the River

Your pickup time depends on where you're staying in Phuket. If you're in Patong, Kata or Karon, our minibus collects you around 07:45 or earlier. Guests from further flung spots like Nai Harn or Rawai start their journey a little earlier, at 07:00, while those near the airport or Mai Khao are picked up closer to 09:15. All transfers are included in your package, so there's nothing extra to pay on the day.

The drive takes you north into the mountains of Phang Nga, roughly an hour and a half from the popular beach areas. The scenery shifts as you leave the tourist corridor behind — limestone cliffs peek through the green, and the air carries that unmistakable river freshness.

By mid-morning, you're at our base, meeting the team and getting fitted for your life jacket and helmet. This is also when you choose your camping zone for the night. The **Family Zone** suits groups of up to four or five, starting at THB 999 per night. If you're after something more intimate, the **Outdoor Zone** accommodates two to three guests from THB 1099, while the **Private Zone** — perfect for couples — starts at THB 1199. Each zone has its own character, and you're welcome to read about the different setups in our guide to [Glamping in Phang Nga: The Zones, the Tents and What's Included](/en/blog/glamping-in-phang-nga-zones-and-whats-included).

## Midday: The Rafting Run

Now for the main event. Whether you've chosen the 5.5 kilometre run or the extended 7.5 kilometre route, the river delivers the same rush — just with a bit more of it on the longer stretch.

Your guide briefs the group at the launch point, explaining paddle commands and safety positions. Then you're in the raft, pushed off from the riverbank, and the current takes hold immediately. The first rapid always catches people off guard — the water surges, the raft bucks, and everyone's paddling hard to keep course. That's the moment the whole trip clicks into focus.

The 5.5 kilometre run takes around four hours with the extended version adding another half hour or so. Between the rapids, the river calms into stretches where you can take in the limestone walls towering on either side, the jungle canopy overhead, and the occasional kingfisher darting across the water. Your guide reads the river ahead, calling out when to paddle hard and when to dig in for the next wave.

Midway through, the raft pulls into a natural eddy for a break. Fresh fruit and cold water are handed round. This is when the silence of the gorge really hits you — no engines, no crowds, just water and jungle.

After the final rapid, you drift to the take-out point, buzzing with that post-river glow. Back at base, you'll freshen up before heading to camp.

## Afternoon: Settling into Camp

The camp sits right beside the river, close enough that you can hear the current from your tent. Depending on your package, you've chosen either the **Stay & Dine** option — a night under canvas with dinner and breakfast included — or one of our combination packages that pair your overnight stay with a rafting session the next morning, either the 5.5 kilometre or 7.5 kilometre run.

Tents are already set up when you arrive, each one with proper bedding, lighting and a private veranda overlooking the water. The Family Zone tents are larger, comfortably fitting a family with children, while the Private Zone offers a more secluded setup for couples wanting a quiet evening.

Late afternoon is unhurried. Wander down to the riverbank, dip your feet in the cool water, or simply sit on your veranda with a fresh coconut — there's always a stack waiting in the camp kitchen. As the light softens toward evening, the jungle comes alive with bird calls and the rustle of creatures settling in for the night.

## Evening: Dinner by the River

Dinner is served at the camp's open-air restaurant, with dishes prepared from local ingredients. Grilled river fish, spicy Thai salads, stir-fried vegetables and sticky rice — nothing fancy, but flavourful and exactly what you need after a day on the water. The team eats together, guides and guests sharing stories from the river. This is when the experience shifts from adventure to something more social and relaxed.

After dinner, there's no pressure to do anything. Some guests gather around the communal area for a drink, others retreat to their tents early. The night sky here is remarkably clear — no city lights to dim the stars. If you're in the Private Zone or Outdoor Zone, you're far enough from other groups to hear nothing but the river.

## Night: The Sound of Water

Sleeping by the river is a sensory experience. The tent canvas muffles the sounds so they're never intrusive, but you never quite lose awareness of the water moving past. It's rhythmic, almost hypnotic, and most guests report sleeping deeper than they have in months. The jungle settles too — occasional night birds, the occasional splash of a fish jumping, but otherwise a profound quiet that city-dwellers often find startling.

Temperatures drop comfortably at night, and the bedding provided is more than adequate. Most guests are asleep by ten, exhausted from the day's paddling and fresh air.

## Morning: Waking in the Jungle

You won't need an alarm. The jungle wakes before you do — birdsong, morning light filtering through the canvas, and the renewed sound of the river as the sun dries the mist off the water.

Breakfast is a simple spread of Thai-style rice porridge, eggs, fresh fruit, toast and coffee. Eat on your veranda if you can, watching the river catch the early light.

If you've booked the **Stay + Raft 5.5** or **Stay + Raft 7.5** package, your second rafting run happens after breakfast — a rare treat, doing the same stretch with the confidence of knowing exactly what lies ahead. The river feels different the second time, more familiar, and you find yourself noticing details you missed the first time through.

By early afternoon, it's time to pack up. The minibus departs for Phuket, and you're back at your hotel by late afternoon, sandy-footed and still buzzing from the night before.

## FAQ

### Can children join the overnight rafting and camping combo?
Yes. Children aged 6 and above can participate in the rafting activities at the standard rates. Children under 6 go free but don't take part in the activities. Our Family Zone tents comfortably accommodate families, and the river runs are suitable for beginners.

### What should I bring for an overnight camping stay?
Pack light, practical clothing — swimwear, quick-dry shirts, sturdy sandals and a change of clothes for the next day. Biodegradable sunscreen and insect repellent are useful. We'll provide bedding, towels and all camping equipment, so there's no need for sleeping bags or tents.

### Is the river safe if I've never rafted before?
Absolutely. No prior experience is needed. Our guides are fully trained, the rapids are graded for beginner-to-intermediate paddlers, and safety briefings are given before every run. Life jackets and helmets are provided and must be worn at all times on the water.

### What happens if it rains?
The river runs year-round, and light rain rarely affects the experience — the jungle shelter above adds atmosphere. In cases of heavy rain or unsafe river conditions, we either rerun the rapids when conditions improve or offer a full refund or free reschedule.

### How far in advance should I book the overnight combo?
The camp has limited tents across the three zones, and peak season fills quickly. Booking at least a few days ahead is recommended, especially for the Private Zone or during weekends. You can check availability and book directly through our [riverside camping](/en#camp-book) page.
