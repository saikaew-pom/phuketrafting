### Article
Planning a Phuket adventure but feeling unsure about what you should actually pay? You're not alone. The tourism scene here can be confusing, with prices all over the place depending on where you book. Let me walk you through what our tours cost, what you get for that price, and why you might see different numbers floating around.

## Our 2026 Tour Prices

We keep things straightforward with six main tour options, all priced clearly with no hidden surprises.

**The Classic Rush** is our most popular choice — 5.5 kilometres of white-water rafting combined with a jungle zipline circuit. At THB 3,000 per person for ages 6 and above, it runs approximately 4 hours and includes everything you need for the day.

Want to add something extra? **The Adventure Combo** gives you the same 5.5 km rafting and zipline, plus your choice of either a 40-minute ATV ride with a fresh coconut or a 30-minute elephant trek. This comes in at THB 3,400 per person and takes roughly 5 hours.

For the full experience, **The Full Option** includes everything — both the ATV and the elephant trek alongside the rafting and zipline. At THB 3,800 per person, it's a full-day adventure.

We also offer extended versions of each tour with 7.5 kilometres of rafting instead. The Classic Rush Extended is THB 3,600, the Adventure Combo Extended is THB 4,000, and the Full Option Extended is THB 4,400.

Children under 6 go free when they don't participate in activities — a nice touch for families. Group sizes range from 2 to 10 people per booking.

## What's Actually Included

Every single one of our tour prices covers the same core inclusions, and I want to be crystal clear about this because it's where the real value sits.

Your white-water rafting adventure includes professional guides, quality safety equipment, and a proper briefing before you hit the water. The jungle zipline circuit takes you through the forest canopy with all gear provided. A Thai lunch is served on-site — nothing fancy, but filling and authentic. And perhaps best of all, round-trip hotel transfers are included from most areas across Phuket.

Speaking of transfers, this is where many travellers get caught out. We've listed pickup zones and times clearly — Patong, Kata, Karon, Kathu, Kamala and several other areas all receive free transfers. Some areas like Nai Harn and Rawai have a small THB 300 surcharge, and Naithon is THB 200. But here's the thing: those transfer costs are clearly stated, so you'll never be surprised.

When you book with us, you're dealing directly with the operator. We're a family-run business that's been doing this since 2002. That means no middleman markup, no commission to pay to a sales agent, and no pressure to upsell you on things you don't need.

## How Payments Work

We keep payment simple. When you reserve, you pay a deposit online to secure your spot. The remaining balance is paid on the day of your tour — either in cash or by card, whatever works for you.

We also offer free cancellation or the ability to reschedule if your plans change, as long as you give us at least 72 hours notice before your departure. And if we ever need to cancel due to weather or safety concerns, you'll receive a full refund or a free reschedule — no arguments, no hassle.

This policy is part of why we ask for the deposit. It holds your place while keeping everything flexible for you.

## Why Street-Agent Pricing Differs

You might walk down any tourist street in Phuket and see flyers offering "rafting tours" at various prices. Some will look cheaper than ours. Here's what's actually happening.

Those prices typically don't include everything we include. Often, the advertised price is just for the rafting activity itself — the transport to the river, the lunch, the zipline, the guide costs, and all the other pieces get added on separately once you're there. Suddenly that "cheap" deal has doubled in price.

Other times, you're paying a commission to the street agent who sold you the ticket. That commission has to come from somewhere, and it either gets built into the price you pay or gets taken out of what the actual operator receives — which sometimes means a lower quality experience on the water.

When you book directly with us, you're cutting out that middle layer. You know exactly what you're paying for, and you get to speak directly with the people running the operation. If you have questions about the tour, the equipment, the river conditions, or anything else — you ask us, not a salesperson who's never actually been rafting.

It's also worth knowing that we don't play pricing games. Our prices are the same whether you book a week in advance or the day before (subject to availability, of course). We don't increase prices during peak season or drop them to undercut everyone else. What you see is what you pay.

## FAQ

### What payment methods do you accept for the deposit?

We accept online payments through our booking system. The balance can be paid in cash or by card on the day of your tour.

### Can I get a refund if the weather is bad?

Yes. If we need to cancel your tour due to weather or safety concerns, you'll receive a full refund or we can reschedule your booking for free.

### Are the prices per person or per group?

All our tour prices are per person. Children under 6 go free when they don't participate in activities.

### Do I need to bring any equipment?

We provide all the safety gear and equipment you need for rafting and ziplining. Just bring swimwear, a change of clothes, and sunscreen. Towels are available at the base.

### How far in advance should I book?

We recommend booking at least a few days ahead to secure your preferred date, especially during peak season. However, we sometimes have availability for next-day tours — it's always worth asking.
