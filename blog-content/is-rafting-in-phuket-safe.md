### Article
Every traveler asks it the moment they hear "white-water rafting." Before they ask about the rapids, the lunch, or the photos — they ask: is this safe?

It's the right question to ask. And it's one we've been answering for twenty-two years, running trips on the Same River in Phang Nga Province, just a short drive from Phuket. Here's the honest answer.

## What actually makes rafting safe

Safety in white-water rafting isn't a single thing. It's a system — and the more pieces you have in place, the more reliable it becomes.

**Professional guides are the backbone.** Every raft on our water carries a minimum of two experienced guides — one at the stern steering and reading the current, one at the bow calling the strokes. Our guides aren't seasonal hires pulled in for peak season. Most have been with us for years, and every single one holds current certifications in river safety and first aid. They know every bend of this stretch of river, how the water behaves after rain, and what the rapids look like in different conditions. That's not something you can fake — it's built over hundreds of runs.

**The equipment matters more than people realise.** We provide high-quality PFDs (personal flotation devices) that are fitted to each guest before you ever touch the water. Helmets are non-negotiable. The rafts themselves are commercial-grade inflatable vessels designed to handle theforce of Class III and IV rapids. Paddles are inspected before every trip. If any piece of gear shows wear, it doesn't go on the water — simple as that.

**The pre-trip briefing sets the tone.** Before any paddle touches river, every group sits through a safety briefing. We cover how to hold the paddle, what commands mean ("forward," "backward," "left turn," "hold"), how to react if you fall out, and most importantly — the golden rule of white-water rafting: keep your feet pointed downstream. We don't rush this. It's not theatre. It's where a lot of the actual safety lives.

**Reading the river is what guides do.** White water isn't random chaos. It follows patterns — the way rocks deflect flow, where hydraulics form (the "holes" that can hold a boat), where the current speeds up or slows. Our guides are reading this continuously as they steer. They choose the line — the safest path through the rapids — in real time. You're not piloting blind. You're following someone who knows the water intimately.

## The honest part: this is an adventure activity

We won't dress this up. White-water rafting is an adventure activity. It involves moving water, rapids, paddling, and physical exertion. There is real risk involved — the same risk that makes it exciting and memorable in the first place.

Rapids are rated on a scale from Class I (easy, almost flat) to Class VI (basically unnavigable). The river we run sits in the Class III to IV range — genuine rapids with real push. You'll get splashed, you'll feel the boat surge forward, and there will be moments where the water looks genuinely serious. That's the point. If it weren't challenging, it wouldn't be worth doing.

Injuries do happen in this sport — from minor bruises and scrapes to, in rare cases, more serious incidents. We've been running since 2002 without a major safety incident, and that record comes from obsessive attention to the basics: never skipping the briefing, never using worn equipment, never letting a guide lead a trip when they're not fully prepared.

What we won't do is promise you "100% safe" or say you'll be "perfectly fine." Nobody who operates responsibly makes those claims — because they aren't true. What we can promise is that we've built every part of our operation around minimising risk, and we've done it long enough to know what we're doing.

## FAQ

### What should I bring on a rafting trip?

Bring a change of clothes, swimwear, a towel, and sturdy water shoes or sandals that stay on your feet (flip-flops tend to wash off in rapids). Sunscreen and a dry bag for valuables are useful. We provide all the safety equipment — PFD, helmet, and paddle.

### Can children join the rafting trips?

Yes — children aged 6 and up are welcome on all our trips. The Classic Rush and Extended Run options are popular with families. Under-6s go free but don't participate in the activity. If you're bringing younger kids, message us to discuss the best option for your family.

### What happens if it rains on the day of my booking?

Light rain doesn't affect the river run — in fact, it can make the experience even more exhilarating. If there's heavy rain that raises water levels dangerously, we monitor conditions closely and will either adjust the route or reschedule your trip. We'll never put you on the water if it's not safe to do so.

### Is prior rafting experience required?

Not at all. No experience is needed for any of our trips. The pre-trip briefing covers everything, and our guides paddle with you throughout — you follow their commands and paddle as directed. First-timers are absolutely welcome on every tour we offer.

### What is the cancellation policy?

You can cancel or reschedule for free up to 72 hours before your departure. If we need to cancel due to weather or safety conditions, you'll receive a full refund or a free reschedule to a date that suits you.
